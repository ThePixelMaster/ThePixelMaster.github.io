Croissant Freezer Breakfast Sandwiches

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Total Time: 30 minutes Yields: 6 sandwiches

Ingredients

EGGS

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 8 large eggs
  • salt, to taste
  • black pepper, to taste

FOR ASSEMBLY

  • 6 smoked Gouda or cheddar cheese slices
  • 6 croissant buns, buttered and toasted
  • 8 ounces cooked beef or turkey bacon

Instructions

MAKE THE EGGS

  1. To a bowl, crack all the eggs in and season with salt and a bit of black pepper. Whisk together well until smooth.

  2. Heat a large non-stick frying pan over low heat and add vegetable oil. Pour in the whisked eggs. Slowly stir with a rubber spatula in circular motions continuously as they cook. The key is to go low and slow. Once the eggs hold their shape turn off the heat to prevent overcooking and set aside.

ASSEMBLE SANDWICHES

  1. To each buttered and toasted bun, add eggs, a slice of cheese, and beef bacon. Close the sandwich and fill in the rest.

  2. Wrap each one in sandwich paper and store it in a ziploc bag or container in the freezer.

  3. To enjoy, pop in the microwave for 90 seconds and enjoy warm!





































Better-Than-Takeout Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Total Time: 30 minutes Yields: 4

Ingredients

FOR THE STEAK:

  • 1 pound flank, NY strip, or ribeye steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

FOR THE VEGETABLE STIR FRY:

  • 1 pound broccoli florets
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons neutral oil, as needed
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste

FOR THE SAUCE:

  • 1/3 cup low sodium beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • pinch of cayenne powder

Instructions

  1. Marinate the steak: In a medium bowl, toss the sliced steak with cornstarch, dark soy sauce, baking soda, white pepper, and salt. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the sauce: In another bowl or measuring cup, whisk beef broth, low sodium soy sauce, brown sugar, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, dark soy sauce, cornstarch, white pepper, and cayenne powder together until smooth. Set aside.

  3. Cook the broccoli: Heat 1 tablespoon neutral oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add broccoli and stir-fry briefly. Add a splash of water, cover, and steam for 2-3 minutes until broccoli is tender-crisp. Remove broccoli and set aside.

  4. Cook the steak: Over medium-high heat, heat the wok or pan until it’s very hot, then add 1 1/2 to 2 tablespoons neutral oil. Add the marinated steak and stir-fry until just cooked through and seared, about 2-3 minutes. Remove and set aside.

  5. Sauté aromatics: Lower the heat to medium-low. Add sesame oil and 1/2 tablespoon neutral oil to the pan. Add garlic and ginger, sauté gently until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  6. Combine: Return broccoli and steak to the pan. Pour in the sauce and toss everything together until the sauce thickens and coats evenly, about 1-2 minutes.

  7. Serve hot with rice or noodles.





































Japanese Creme Filled Sandwiches

Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Total Time: 40 minutes Yields: 10

Ingredients

BREAD

  • 5 Japanese milk bread slices
  • vegetable oil, for frying

WHIPPED CREME

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

CUSTARD (optional)

  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

OTHER

  • strawberries, stem removed and cut in half
  • granulated sugar

Instructions

MAKE THE CUSTARD (OPTIONAL)

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat whole milk, vanilla extract, and salt over medium-low until just warm.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together cornstarch, sugar, and egg yolks until smooth.

  3. Gradually pour the warm milk into the egg mixture while whisking continuously to temper the eggs.

  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened to a custard-like consistency.

  5. Remove from heat and stir in the butter until fully melted and incorporated.

  6. Transfer the custard to a bowl, cover the surface with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming, and chill in the refrigerator until completely cool.

MAKE THE WHIPPED CREAM

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract.

  2. Whisk the mixture until stiff peaks form.

  3. Cover the whipped cream and store it in the refrigerator until ready to use.

FRY THE BREAD

  1. Heat a shallow layer of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.

  2. Fry each slice of Japanese milk bread until golden and crispy on both sides, flipping carefully to prevent tearing.

  3. Transfer the fried bread to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

ASSEMBLE THE SANDWICHES

  1. For Strawberry Whipped Cream Sandwiches:

  2. Cut each fried bread slice in half and gently open each piece to create a pocket.

  3. Fill the pocket generously with whipped cream and top with three strawberry halves.

For Crème Brûlée Sandwiches:

  1. Cut each fried bread slice in half and gently open each piece to create a pocket.

  2. Fill the pocket about two-thirds of the way with whipped cream, leaving room for the custard.

  3. Spoon custard into the remaining one-third of the pocket.

  4. Sprinkle an even layer of granulated sugar over the custard and brûlée it with a kitchen torch until caramelized and golden. Allow the caramelized sugar to cool and harden slightly before serving.
