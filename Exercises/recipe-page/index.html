Banana Bread Pancakes

Prep Time: 10 Minutes Cook Time 15 Minutes

Total Time: 25 Minutes Yields: 6 Pancakes

Fluffy, cozy, and full of banana bread flavor, these pancakes are the perfect weekend treat. The edges get golden and buttery while the centers stay soft and full of melty chocolate chips. Stack them high, drizzle with maple syrup, and watch them disappear in seconds.

Ingredients You’ll Need

A handful of pantry staples make these pancakes extra special:

  • Ripe bananas: The secret to deep banana flavor and natural sweetness
  • Buttermilk: Keeps the pancakes fluffy and light and airy.
  • Brown sugar: Adds caramel notes that pair perfectly with banana.
  • Baking powder + baking soda: Helps the pancakes rise and stay soft.
  • Butter: For that golden, flavorful edge (you can use oil, but butter adds richness). You can also brown it for extra flavor in the pancakes.
  • Walnuts and mini chocolate chips: The perfect textural combo and traditional add ins to banana bread — crunchy, melty, and cozy. Feel free to swap with any nuts you like such as pecans or leave them out.

    • Tip: If you only have regular milk, you can make a quick homemade buttermilk by mixing 1 cup of milk with 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar and letting it sit for 5 minutes.

    How To Make It

    Mash the bananas In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas until mostly smooth with a few small chunks left for texture is totally ok.

    Whisk the wet ingredients Add in the egg, melted butter, vanilla extract, and cold buttermilk. Whisk until smooth and creamy.

    Mix the dry ingredients In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

    Combine everything Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and use a rubber spatula to fold until just combined.

    Don’t overmix! A few lumps in the batter are perfect — that’s what keeps pancakes fluffy instead of dense.

    Cook the pancakes Heat a nonstick pan or griddle over medium heat and add about 1/2 tablespoon of butter to melt. Once it starts bubbling, scoop about 1/2 cup of batter for each pancake (or smaller if you prefer minis).

    As soon as the batter hits the pan, sprinkle a few walnuts and mini chocolate chips on top — they’ll melt and toast slightly once you flip. Cook until bubbles form on top and the edges start to look set, then flip and cook until golden brown.

    Tip: If you’re using a griddle, you can cook 3–4 pancakes at a time, which makes breakfast so much quicker (and perfect for big batches).

    Variation: Want to make them a little extra banana-forward? Add a few thin slices of banana directly onto each pancake before flipping — they’ll caramelize beautifully.
